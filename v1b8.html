Local truth beats global intention

Intention is invisible to the system.

You can want a network to do something with every ounce of sincerity available, and none of that desire reaches the containers. They do not know your goal. They do not know the shape you are aiming for. They only know what arrives at their inputs this frame.

This creates a gap that feels personal at first.

People say things like “it knows what I mean” or “it should understand this.” That language assumes a shared context. What exists instead is a collection of local agreements. Each container responds to the immediate situation touching it and nothing beyond that boundary.

A node does not know it is part of a larger plan. It does not know it sits inside a performance, an installation, or a concept deck. It knows the type, structure, and timing of the signal it receives. That knowledge resets every frame.

This is why fixing problems at the wrong scale never works. Adjusting something downstream to satisfy an upstream wish only hides the disagreement. The container will keep doing what it is built to do with whatever it receives, even if the result contradicts your story.

Global intention lives in your head. Local truth lives in the wires.

When a result surprises you, it is not being clever. It is being faithful. The system is honoring the exact conditions presented to it, not the conditions you thought you were presenting.

This is also why explanations that rely on meaning fall apart. “This represents motion” or “this stands for energy” carries no weight inside the network. Representation is a human overlay applied after the fact. The containers only care whether a value changes, repeats, arrives late, or arrives malformed.

Debugging becomes less emotional once this is accepted. The question shifts from “why is it doing this” to “what does this container see right now.” Answers show up faster when they are grounded in immediate inputs rather than hoped-for outcomes.

This local focus is not a limitation. It is what allows systems to stay stable under pressure. Each part does its job without negotiating meaning. The result emerges from alignment, not interpretation.

Intention still matters, just not where people expect. It belongs in how you arrange responsibilities, not in what you wish the system would infer.
