How space becomes images

You have something that occupies space now.

It has position. It has size. It responds to numbers. What it does not have is appearance.

Space alone is invisible here.

Start from the network you already built.

There should be geometry feeding through a Transform node, reacting to changing values.

Geometry does not draw itself. It describes where something could be. To be seen, it needs a witness.

Move your mouse to an empty space to the right of your geometry.

Press Tab.

Type one of the following and place it to the right:

  • Camera
  • Light

If you add only one, start with Camera.

Nothing changes yet.

A camera without a renderer sees nothing. A renderer without a camera has no point of view.

Press Tab again.

Type Render and place it to the right of everything.

This node turns space into pixels. It does not create geometry. It observes it.

Wire your geometry chain into the Render node.

Wire the Camera into the camera input of the Render.

Open the viewer on the Render node.

This is the first time something looks like an image.

What you are seeing is not the geometry. It is a decision about the geometry, made from a particular position, at a particular moment, under a particular set of conditions.

Change the numbers driving the geometry. The image changes.

Change the camera. The image changes again.

The geometry itself has not learned anything. It is being interpreted repeatedly.

Move the camera node around in the network.

Then change its position parameters.

Nothing downstream cares where the camera lives in the network. It only cares about what the camera reports.

This is the rule.

Space becomes images only when someone is looking, and the act of looking is just another signal in the system.
