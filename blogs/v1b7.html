What moves: signal flow without the myth

This guide explains TouchDesigner operator families explained in plain language. It focuses on practical decisions you can make while building and debugging TouchDesigner networks.

The word “flow” picks up baggage fast.

People imagine motion as something dramatic. Lines pulsing. Values surging. When that spectacle does not appear, the assumption follows that nothing is happening.

Flow is simpler than that.

Something enters a container. The container evaluates it. Something leaves, or nothing does. That evaluation repeats every frame.

A steady signal is still flowing. Each frame asks the same question and receives the same answer.

Wiring allows flow. A connection is permission, not pressure. Until something upstream changes, downstream containers have nothing to respond to.

Networks can look busy but behave inert. Boxes everywhere. Wires crossing. No demand.

Containers do not interpret intention. They respond to type and structure.

Flow can originate anywhere a value changes over time. A timer. A sensor. A file updating.

One signal can travel multiple paths at once. There is no notion of depletion.

When flow stops, outputs flatten. The fix involves tracing change backward to its origin.

Flow is the condition that allows anything downstream to matter.

FAQ

What are TOP CHOP SOP DAT and COMP in TouchDesigner?

They are operator families. TOPs process images, CHOPs process channels, SOPs process geometry, DATs handle data tables and text, and COMPs organize networks.

How do I know which operator family to use?

Choose based on the type of data you are working with. Images use TOPs, numeric channels use CHOPs, geometry uses SOPs, structured data uses DATs.

Can CHOP data control TOP visuals?

Yes. CHOP channels can be exported or referenced to control parameters inside TOP operators.
