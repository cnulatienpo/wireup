A minimal network has only what is necessary to produce behavior.

For beginners, complexity hides understanding. Every additional operator creates another dependency to reason about.

A simple setup reveals cause and effect clearly.

A source produces data. A transform modifies it. An output displays it.

That chain is enough to learn most core ideas.

When learning, avoid stacking layers of modulation, conversion, and switching too early.

Three to six nodes are often sufficient to understand interaction.

Minimal does not mean limited.

It means intentional.

If something breaks, fewer nodes make tracing upstream faster.

If something behaves strangely, fewer dependencies make debugging easier.

Minimal networks teach structure instead of decoration.

Once structure is understood, expansion becomes deliberate rather than reactive.

Large systems are built from small understandable pieces.

A beginner who masters small patches progresses faster than one who builds sprawling networks without clarity.

Start small. Confirm behavior. Expand carefully.

Growth should follow comprehension, not frustration.

FAQ

what is feedback loop in touchdesigner

A feedback loop feeds prior output back into current evaluation, creating history-dependent behavior. That transforms a static setup into a dynamic system over time. The official docs and examples on feedback operators explain the concept.

why feedback causes unexpected growth or decay

Loops amplify small biases, so tiny offsets can accumulate into major drift. This is normal systems behavior, not random failure. Docs on feedback, normalization, and clamping discuss stability controls.

how to think about stability in realtime feedback

Stability comes from gain, damping, bounds, and reset logic choices. You are designing system dynamics, not just a visual effect. The official docs on feedback networks provide the right conceptual vocabulary.

is feedback same as delay

Delay is often one ingredient, but feedback is about recursive influence across time. It changes system identity by making history matter. The docs distinguish simple buffering from closed-loop structures.
