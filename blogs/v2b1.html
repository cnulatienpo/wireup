Where things are allowed to start

This page assumes TouchDesigner is open and you are looking at an empty network. Nothing is selected. Nothing is wired.

The first thing this system respects is insistence. Something that keeps producing values whether anyone cares or not.

Move your mouse into the network editor.

Press Tab.

In the menu that appears, type one of the following and press Enter:

  • Timer
  • Noise
  • LFO
  • Audio Device In (if a microphone exists)

Any of these is acceptable. Pick one. Place it near the center of the network.

You have added something that refuses to stop. Even if the numbers look boring, they advance every frame. That advancement is not optional.

Nothing else in the network knows this yet.

Press Tab again.

Type one of the following and place it to the right of the first node:

  • Math
  • Lag
  • Filter

These nodes accept numbers and respond to them. They do not create numbers. They wait.

Click the small output square on the right side of the first node.

Drag a wire to the input square on the left side of the second node.

Release.

Something new has happened.

The second node is now obligated to respond every frame. It did not change its personality. It simply began receiving pressure.

If you open the viewer on the second node, you will see numbers moving or repeating. That repetition is the work.

Break the wire by selecting it and pressing Delete.

Whatever behavior you were seeing stopped instantly.

No reset occurred. No history was undone. The obligation disappeared and the system reorganized itself around that absence.

Reconnect the wire.

Then replace the first node with a different source from the earlier list.

Notice what stays the same.

The second node behaves exactly as before. Only the pressure feeding it has changed. This is the shape of every setup you will build from here on.

A source insists. Everything else responds. Without insistence, nothing earns attention.
