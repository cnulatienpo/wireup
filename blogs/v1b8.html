Local truth beats global intention

This guide explains time as a signal in TouchDesigner in plain language. It focuses on practical decisions you can make while building and debugging TouchDesigner networks.

Intention is invisible to the system.

You can want a network to behave a certain way, but containers only know what arrives at their inputs this frame.

This creates a gap that feels personal at first.

Each container responds to the immediate situation touching it and nothing beyond that boundary.

A node does not know it is part of a larger plan. It knows the type, structure, and timing of the signal it receives.

Adjusting something downstream to satisfy an upstream wish only hides the disagreement. Containers respond to actual inputs, not intention.

Global intention lives in your head. Local truth lives in the wires.

When a result surprises you, it is honoring the exact conditions presented to it.

Representation is human interpretation. Containers only care whether a value changes, repeats, arrives late, or arrives malformed.

Debugging improves when you ask what this container sees right now.

Each part does its job without negotiating meaning. Results emerge from alignment, not interpretation.

Intention belongs in how responsibilities are arranged, not in what you expect the system to infer.

FAQ

How is time treated as data in TouchDesigner?

Time is available as a signal through timeline components and CHOPs. It can be referenced, modified, and routed like other data streams.

Why does my animation depend on frame rate?

If calculations are tied directly to frames rather than time values, changes in frame rate can alter behavior. Using time-based logic helps stabilize motion.

What is time slicing in TouchDesigner?

Time slicing allows operators to process multiple samples per frame, improving responsiveness for time-dependent data.
