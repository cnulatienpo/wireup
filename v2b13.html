Why this stops feeling like software

Somewhere along the way, the questions changed.

Early on, everything revolved around names. What is this node called. What menu is it in. Which operator am I supposed to know.

Those questions fade once the system starts behaving as a whole.

Right now, you are no longer assembling features. You are maintaining conditions.

Motion exists because something insists. Structure exists because something remembers. Images exist because something is watching. Decisions exist because something is allowed through. Stability exists because pressure is shaped.

None of those ideas belong to software.

They belong to systems.

Look at your network one last time.

Do not zoom in.

You are not meant to read it line by line.

You recognize it the way you recognize a room you know well. You know where things tend to live. You know which areas are sensitive. You know where change will echo.

That recognition did not come from memorization. It came from repeated cause and effect.

This is why the system resists step-by-step mastery. Steps imply sequence. What you built responds to relationship.

When something breaks now, you do not panic. You isolate pressure. You follow influence. You adjust responsibility.

That is not using software. That is operating a rig.

Software usually asks you what you want to make. This asks you what you are willing to maintain.

If you remove one piece, something else will compensate. If you add something reckless, the system will tell you immediately.

This is why people describe the experience as physical. Not because it involves hardware, but because it responds like something with mass.

You do not finish systems like this. You leave them running.

At some point, you stop thinking about TouchDesigner at all.

You think in signals, pressure, thresholds, and agreements.

That is the real transition.
